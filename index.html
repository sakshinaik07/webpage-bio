About me

Hi! I’m Sakshi, a Master’s student at Texas A&M University. My background is in engineering, but I’m really interested in how people experience technology and how design choices can make content easier to use for more people.

What I’m into

  • Building clear, well-structured digital content
  • Learning how accessibility improves usability for everyone

Why accessibility matters to me

Accessibility is not “extra”—it’s part of quality. Small details like headings, labels, and keyboard-friendly layouts can remove barriers and help people participate more independently. I want to learn how to design in a way that is inclusive and practical.

My accessibility interests

I’m especially interested in improvements that are easy to understand, test, and maintain. I like changes that make content clearer for everyone, including people using screen readers or keyboard navigation.

Topics I want to explore more

  1. Keyboard navigation, visible focus, and predictable interaction
  2. Semantic HTML (headings and landmarks)
  3. Clear language and reducing unnecessary complexity

Team project fit

I’d love to collaborate on a project where we can identify issues, make improvements, and explain why those changes matter. I’m happy to help with testing, structure, and writing clear instructions.

How I can contribute

  • Keyboard-only testing and focus order checks
  • Improving semantics (headings, landmarks, labels)
  • Writing clear, friendly microcopy
